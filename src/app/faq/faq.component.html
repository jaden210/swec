Common Questions
Tips to really make this work, coming from an OD:
As a patient, it can be quite complicated and confusing to keep track of everything when it comes to contacts, and I would never expect ANY patient to know how they can get the best value. Most automatically assume they can find them cheaper online, from 1-800 or from a big box store. Unless you have your contact lens pricing sky high, this is simply not true.

You are the doctor. Eye health has to remain top priority. A daily lens should be worn once, a 2-week lens for 2 weeks, and a monthly for a month, no matter what their previous eye care provider has told them or what they think their eyes can tolerate.

Start in the exam room. Don't be pushy but be transparent with the cost and what to expect, and now you can physically show the dollar value of purchasing a year supply of contact lenses from your practice. Show them how you can maximize their vision plan benefit coupled with the higher manufacturer rebates and truly HOW IN-EXPENSIVE IT IS FOR A FULL YEAR SUPPLY!

Patients often remark they have always assumed they would need to take out a small loan to afford to wear daily disposable lenses, but are pleasantly surprised to see that is not the case.

You are automatically creating confusion when you or your staff ask “How many boxes of contacts do you want?” Keep in mind, everyone wins when your patients get a year supply of contacts from you.

OUR GOAL AS A COMPANY IS TO GET RID OF THE CONTACT LENS HEADACHE YOU CURRENTLY HAVE IN YOUR PRACTICE. Show your patients you are looking out for the best interest of their eye health AND wallet.
Who is LensWindow for?
Private practice optometrists or optometry/ophthalmology group settings that prescribe and sell contact lenses.
Wait, what does it even do?
LensWindow is a customizable contact lens database tool that easily combines your unique practice pricing, manufacturer rebate amounts, and vision insurance/plan benefit in an easy to understand quote that can be printed off or sent (.pdf form) via email to your patients. See example here.
Why should I care?
If you are OK with most or all your patients leaving your clinic to go buy contacts elsewhere, you shouldn't. If you are interested in having them buy them from your practice, but haven't been doing a good job of it, or just want to take your contact lens practice to the next level, you have come to the right place.
How can I implement this in my busy practice when I don't have time for something new?
When you sign up, the default lens database will be filled with the lenses most of us already prescribe and recommend. Someone you already pay within the walls of your practice knows how much you charge per box. Have them update your pricing in the software, and off you go. We recommend adding a Lenswindow icon to the toolbar or desktop shortcut in every exam room and every staff work station so the whole team can get to your pricing with a single click.
Do I have to do a lengthy staff training when I sign up?
Absolutely not. The beauty of this software is the simplicity of it. After originally setting your practice pricing and manufacturer rebate amounts:
  1. Click on the manufacturer from the Quick Search icon
  2. Click on the lens
  3. Add in vision insurance contribution if applicable
And thats it! You have a price break-down with your logo, letterhead, and contact information.
What if I want to change my lens pricing?
Manufacturer programs and their cost to you may vary from time to time. With the Edit Lens feature, you are in control. We recommend having someone from your office do an audit once per quarter to make sure you are up to date. IF YOU CHANGE THE PRICE FROM ONE WORKSTATION, IT WILL AUTOMATICALLY UPDATE ON THE REST.
What if the manufacturers change their rebate amounts?
Ah yes, probably the single most frustrating game in the industry. Nothing like carrying inventory for the “price lock-in program” just to have the rug pulled out from under you when rebate amounts get cut in half or disappear completely. You will find and can change the rebate amounts under the Edit Lens feature (same place as pricing).
What if a new lens comes to market?
Chances are, as a private-practice owner that does a ton of contact lens volume, contact lens reps want YOU to have the first fitting sets so you can get them on your patients eyes. We will keep up to date on the big 4 (Alcon, B&L, Johnson &Johnson, Coopervision) as best we can. If we aren't quick enough, the Add a Lens feature will allow you to get the new lens into your practice database on your own.
If I have feedback and want you to change something on the print out to accommodate how we already talk to patients about contacts, is that an option?
This is a great question. The quote is designed the way it is BECAUSE IT WORKS, and we highly recommend adjusting the way you talk to patients rather than have us adjust the format. That being said, we will do our best as long as it is within reason. It's also worth exploring what's already available for modification on the quote. Custom text can be added to the bottom half and industry terms can be modified.
